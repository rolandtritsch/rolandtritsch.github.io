Remote-First ... is easy!

2022-10-01 Management Community Culture Remote TEDN Thoughts Tips & Tricks

These days we all try to get our head around remote-first and how to run and build remote-first engineering organisations.

What is it? How to get it right? What to do? What not to do?

If we go back to first principles we all want the same: An engineering organisation that is effective and efficient, that builds the right thing and builds it right and that allows great engineers to do there best work (and grow and have fun while doing all of the above).

Now, here comes the controversial idea: For that to happen in a remote-first “way” you do not (really) have to (totally) reinvent the wheel.

Right now, there are lots of blog posts that suggest that remote first is difficult and a big problem and that you have to fundamentally change what you are doing and how you do it.

I would like to challenge this assumption a little bit.

There are a couple of foundational skills that you need to perfect if/when you want to deliver on the first principles above. some of these are …

  • People-Management
  • Product-Management
  • Project-Management
  • Descision-Making
  • ???

But/And … that does not mean that you have to come up with a totally new way of People Management. Instead I am suggesting that you do good and right People Management and then “adjust” it to turn it into Remote-First People-Management. 80% of what makes good People-Management good, will also make good Remote-First People-Management good (e.g. 1-1s, Performance-Reviews & Growth-Plans, Communication, Transparency, Engagement, …). You now just need to figure out how the Remote-First variation of your best version of People-Management looks like (e.g. Remote-First 1:1s).

Even better for some of these areas we already have access to very good research. For instance we all know how good decision making looks like (e.g. KiE). Again frameworks like this cover 80% of what is good and right about decision making. You just need to complement it with a little bit of “remote-first” secret-sauce.

Building and running a remote-first company can be challenging. Not because it is difficult, but because it is different and most of us have very little experience with it. The good news is you do not have to reinvent the wheel. You just have to adjust it (go from spokes to carbon-fiber).

This Master-Class Series touches on some aspects of this adjustment.

The first one will be on Decision Making.How is Remote-First Decision Making different from Office-First Decision Making and what to do about it.
