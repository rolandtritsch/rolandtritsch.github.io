Circle of Life - The other one

2025-04-10 Personal Culture Politics Thoughts

We are living in interesting times.

Climate-change, over-population, social-injustice, … are just some of the global problems we are facing (and actually have been facing for a while).

Recently you may have started asking yourself what the way forward might look like.

One model suggests that we need to and will work together to find global and fair solutions that create lives worth-while living for everybody (and that minimize the impact of the climate-change that we cannot prevent from happing anymore). With that model privilege creates a responsibility to help and support those that are less-privileged.

Another model suggests that everybody will be better off, if we focus on building “local” strength (per country, per city, per community, per family, per individual) and the goal is to first of all find solutions for the people in these local boundaries. This model accepts that living on the planet is a zero-sum game and that for me to have a better life somebody else needs to have a less-good life. There is also acceptance for privilege to be a fact of live and that the less-privileged need to work hard(er) to overcome their disadvantage.

There are probably 10 other models to consider.

I personally have a preference to life in a world that is more aligned with the former/first model.

Right now I am more interested to understand how these models (let’s call them value-/believe-systems) come into existence.

And here the observation is that it is (maybe) circular.

And maybe can be compared with a marriage.

First there are good times. And both enjoy the good times. But the good times also make you lazy and weak and you forget how to work on the marriage and fight for the marriage. And then you end up with/in bad times. In the worst case it will kill the marriage. In the best case you rediscover and redevelop and relearn the skills and capabilities you need to make the marriage work (again). And then you will enjoy another period of good times. And then …

This phenomena is not new. It was first observed/discovered and discussed by ???. The premise is that …

  • Strong people create good times
  • Good times create weak people
  • Weak people create bad/difficult times
  • Bad/Difficult times create/forge strong people
  • (And these) Strong people will create good times (again)

Question is: How long is one of these “loops” (from good times to good times). If you think about the rise and fall of civilisations/empires (the Inka, the Egytians, the Greeks, the Romans, the Portuguese/Spanish, …) you are talking about hundreds of years. On a smaller/shorter scale you can say that weak people created World-War II and strong people fixed it (did win the war and rebuild Europe from the ashes).

What’s bad about this is that (apparently) there always needs to be some suffering, before the next generation of strong leaders emerges.

So the question is: Where are we right now in this circle of life? Unfortunately my guess is that for the next 10-20-50 years we will experience some bad times (before it will get better again).

Let’s work together to make this period of bad time as short as possible.
